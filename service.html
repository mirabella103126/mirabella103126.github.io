QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

Service FAQs

Who can attend the service?

Friends and family of all faiths, and members of the Rodeph Shalom community are invited to attend services on Saturday. No prior knowledge or special preparation is needed.

What is a Bat Mitzvah?

A Bat Mitzvah marks the time when a Jewish girl takes on the responsibilities and privileges of Jewish adulthood. The words also refer to the person herself—Mirabella is becoming a Bat Mitzvah—and we’ll celebrate the milestone with her as she helps lead the congregation in prayer and reads from the Torah.

What happens at the synagogue?

We’ll gather for a Shabbat morning service filled with prayer, music, Torah reading, and reflections. Mirabella will take an active role in leading parts of the service. If this is your first Jewish service, don’t worry: everything you need will be provided, and you’re welcome to participate at whatever level feels comfortable.

What should I wear?

Please wear attire appropriate for a synagogue—festive and polished, but it doesn’t need to be formal. Shoulders should be covered, and we suggest avoiding very short hemlines.

What is Mirabella’s Torah portion?

Mirabella is expected to read from Vayera, the weekly Torah portion for this Shabbat. It includes stories about hospitality, faith, family, and the responsibility to act with compassion and justice. We’ll share more about the passages Mirabella has chosen to explore as the day approaches.

What is a mitzvah project?

A mitzvah project is a meaningful act of service connected to becoming a Bat Mitzvah. It gives Mirabella a way to put Jewish values into action by supporting a cause she cares about. We’ll share details about her project here soon.
